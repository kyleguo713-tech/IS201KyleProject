Why China?

China offers one of the richest travel experiences on Earth. From the winding stone of the Great Wall to the quiet courtyards of the Forbidden City, history is not something you read about—it is something you walk through. Dynasties, empires, and revolutions have left behind architecture, art, and stories that span thousands of years.

Beyond monuments, China is a feast for the senses. Regional cuisines range from delicate dim sum to fiery Sichuan hot pot, and tea culture runs as deep as the mountains. Traditional festivals, calligraphy, opera, and modern art cities like Shanghai show how old and new coexist side by side.

Landscapes are just as diverse: karst peaks reflected in rivers, bamboo forests, high plateaus, and neon skylines. Whether you are drawn to ancient history, world-class food, or dramatic scenery, China rewards curiosity at every turn.

Top Destinations by Region

  1. Northern China
    • Beijing — Home of the Great Wall and Forbidden City
    • Xi'an — The ancient Silk Road capital, home of Terracotta Army
  2. Eastern China
    • Shanghai — Modern skyline meets historic waterfront
    • Hangzhou — Famous for West Lake and tea culture
  3. Southern China
    • Guilin — Stunning karst mountains and Li River
    • Guangzhou — Gateway to China, vibrant food scene
  4. Western China
    • Chengdu — Giant pandas and spicy Sichuan food
    • Zhangjiajie — Floating mountains that inspired Avatar
